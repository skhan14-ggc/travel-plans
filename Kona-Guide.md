# Kona and Hawaii — A Visitor's Guide

Hawaii’s Big Island and the Kona coast offer a unique blend of dramatic volcanic landscapes, sun-drenched beaches, rich cultural history, and world-class coffee. Kona — technically the leeward district on the island of Hawai‘i — is famous for its golden sunsets, clear waters, and the small towns that sit between lava fields and lush uplands. Visitors come for outdoor adventure and slow-paced island time: mornings spent wandering coffee farms and afternoons snorkeling in protected bays, evenings tasting fresh seafood and local brews while the sky blushes orange over the Pacific. The island’s contrasts are striking — black lava rock touching bright sand, cultivated coffee terraces above windswept coastlines, and quiet cultural sites alongside lively farmers’ markets. Kona’s microclimate is warm and dry, making it ideal for seaside activities, while nearby higher elevations host cool, cloud-shrouded forests and stargazing observatories. History is also tangible here: sacred places and petroglyphs recall centuries of Hawaiian life, and local traditions — from hula to canoe building — remain vibrant. Food is central to the Kona experience: farm-to-table freshness, tropical fruit, and the world-famous Kona coffee. Whether you come for adrenaline — diving with manta rays, surfing rolling breaks, hiking ancient trails — or relaxation — spa treatments, beach lounging, and sunset sails — Kona delivers an unmistakable sense of place rooted in aloha and ʻāina (land).

## Things to do and sights (Kona & nearby)

- Kealakekua Bay / Captain Cook Monument — top snorkeling and kayaking spot with abundant marine life.
- Night manta-ray snorkel or dive off Kona Coast — one of the most famous manta experiences in the world.
- Kona coffee farm tours in Holualoa and Kona Coffee Belt — tastings and plantation walks.
- Puʻuhonua o Hōnaunau National Historical Park — an important cultural refuge and historic site.
- Hapuna Beach and other white-sand beaches for swimming and sunbathing.
- Mauna Kea summit stargazing (guided tours recommended) — extraordinary high-altitude skies.
- Kaloko-Honokōhau National Historical Park — coastal archaeology, fishponds, sea turtles.
- Snorkeling at Honaunau and Keauhou, scuba diving, and boat trips to secluded bays.
- Helicopter or aerial tours to see lava flows, valleys, and waterfalls.
- Hikes: Pololū Valley (north shore), Puʻu Hinai (local ridges), and coastal trails.

## Things to eat

- Kona coffee — freshly brewed or as espresso — a must-try local specialty.
- Poke — raw-cured local fish with soy, sea salt, and island flavors.
- Kalua pig — traditionally roasted in an imu (underground oven) and tender-smoked.
- Loco moco — rice, hamburger patty, fried egg, and rich gravy.
- Shave ice — flavored ice with sweet syrups, often topped with condensed milk or azuki beans.
- Fresh tropical fruit — mango, papaya, lychee, and starfruit from local markets.
- Malasadas — Portuguese-style fried doughnuts, a beloved island treat.
- Fresh-caught grilled fish and seafood at local eateries.

## Things to try

- Take a Kona coffee tasting and learn about bean processing.
- Book a guided manta-ray night snorkel or dive (safety briefing and permits as required).
- Join a cultural luau to experience hula, mele (song), and traditional food.
- Try stand-up paddleboarding or kayaking in calm morning waters.
- Go on a fishing charter for deep-sea or bottom fishing.
- Drive the scenic Mamalahoa/University route to taste local food trucks and stop at overlooks.
- Explore local farmers’ markets and try island-made chocolate, honey, and macadamia treats.
